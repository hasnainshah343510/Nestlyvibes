IKEA Hack: Brass Mirror Gallery Wall

5 min read · IKEA · DIY · Weekend Project

The IKEA LOTS mirror is $12 and, on its own, unremarkable — a plain round mirror in a thin frame that most people walk past in the store without a second look. Bought in a set of five and arranged asymmetrically with a coat of brass spray paint, it becomes the kind of gallery wall that stops the scroll on Pinterest and gets asked about by literally everyone who visits the room.

This is one of the highest-value hacks in budget decorating, because the cost-to-impact ratio is almost unmatched. A comparable brass mirror cluster bought pre-made from a home decor retailer runs $150-250. This version, including paint and mounting hardware, comes in under $50 — and takes one afternoon rather than a special order and a wait.

What You'll Need

  • 5× round wall mirrors (a matching set works better than mismatched ones for this specific look)
  • Brass or gold metallic spray paint — one can is enough for five small frames
  • Command strips (renter-friendly, no drilling required)
  • Painter's tape, to protect the mirror glass while spraying the frame
  • An afternoon — mostly drying time, not active work

Prepping the Mirrors

Before any paint touches the frames, cover the mirror glass completely with painter's tape and a sheet of newspaper or cardboard cut to size. Overspray on glass is difficult to fully remove, and it's the single most common mistake people make on this project — rushing the prep step to get to the fun part.

Once covered, apply two to three light coats of brass spray paint rather than one heavy coat. Light coats dry faster, avoid drips, and build a more even, professional-looking finish. Let each coat dry fully (check the can for recommended time, usually 10-15 minutes) before applying the next.

Arranging the Wall

Lay the mirrors out on the floor first, in front of the wall where they'll eventually hang — never skip this step and go straight to mounting, because gallery walls are almost impossible to get right by eye alone once pieces are already on Command strips.

Cluster three mirrors tightly together, with only an inch or two of space between them, then position the remaining two slightly apart from the main cluster — one above, one to the side. This asymmetry is what makes the arrangement read as intentionally designed rather than evenly, almost accidentally, spaced. Perfectly even grids tend to look more like a display shelf than a curated wall.

Take a photo of the floor layout on your phone before mounting anything. It becomes your reference so you don't second-guess spacing once mirrors start going up on the wall.

Mounting Without Damage

Command strips rated for mirrors (check the weight limit on the packaging against your mirror's actual weight) hold this arrangement without any drilling, which matters if you're renting. Apply strips to the back of each frame at two points for anything larger than a small saucer-sized mirror, and press firmly against the wall for the full recommended time before letting go — usually 30 seconds per strip.

Styling the Wall Once It's Up

Once your mirrors are hung, add warmth underneath with layered textiles — see our Boho Bedroom Under $50 guide — or go further into the brass trend with our Neo Deco style breakdown. The mirrors reflect ambient light from candles or string lights particularly well, which is worth keeping in mind when you're deciding what furniture sits beneath the wall.
